# CrystalBio Field Team App: Before / After Case Study Draft

> Working draft for a LinkedIn post or portfolio note once the project is shipped. Replace placeholders before publishing.

## Short version

A client had sales and service agents working in the field, but the business did not have a clear daily view of what was actually happening outside the office.

The work existed. The updates existed somewhere in calls, WhatsApp messages, memory, and manual follow-ups. But the information was scattered.

So we turned the field workflow into a simple mobile-first app:

- Field agents can log in from their phone.
- They can check in and check out with location.
- Sales agents can submit visit updates.
- Service agents can submit service work updates.
- Agents can request leave.
- Admins can see team-wise daily, weekly, and monthly reports.
- Admins can approve leave and review everyone’s field activity in one place.

The goal was not to build “another form.”

The goal was to make field reporting easy enough for non-technical users to actually use every day.

## Before

The client had sales and service agents visiting customers in the field.

But the company was missing visibility into:

- Which agent visited which customer
- What happened during each visit
- Whether a sales follow-up was needed
- Whether a service issue required office support or parts
- Who had checked in for field work
- Who had not updated anything that day
- Leave requests and approval status
- Daily, weekly, and monthly performance summaries

The team had forms and scattered communication, but not a clean operating system for field work.

Common problems:

- Information was delayed or incomplete
- Managers had to ask for updates manually
- Reports depended on someone collecting data later
- Field agents had to remember details after the visit
- Non-technical users could get overwhelmed by long forms
- Admins could not quickly compare attendance, visits, follow-ups, and leave

## After

We designed a mobile-first field operations app for sales agents, service agents, and admins.

### Agent side

Agents get a simple phone-friendly interface with clear actions:

- Login
- Check in
- Sales visit
- Service visit
- Attendance
- Leave request
- My reports

The design keeps the experience simple:

- Big readable buttons
- Short labels
- No cluttered dashboards for field users
- Forms broken into practical steps
- Required fields kept minimal at the start
- Details can be added later when the agent has time
- Camera and upload options for proof/photos
- Clear saved status after submission

### Admin side

Admins get a separate view after login where they can see:

- Active agents
- Missing check-ins
- Sales and service visit counts
- Pending follow-ups
- Leave requests
- Person-wise reports
- Daily, weekly, and monthly summaries

Admins can also submit their own field forms without logging out, because owners/managers may sometimes do field work too.

## Key product decisions

### 1. One clean login, not separate role confusion

At first, the login screen had separate Agent and Admin choices.

That felt unnecessary.

The better approach: one clean login. The user’s account decides what access they get.

If someone is an admin, they see admin tools. If they also need to submit field work, they can do that inside the same login.

### 2. Field reporting should not feel like a long form

The agents are not highly technical users. So the app should not feel like filling a government form or a spreadsheet.

The design direction became:

- Quick actions first
- Small sections
- Plain language
- Save early
- Add details later
- Make the next step obvious

### 3. Attendance and visits are separate

Check-in/check-out is for starting and ending the field day.

Sales and service visits are separate work entries.

This avoids confusing attendance with customer visit reporting.

### 4. Reports should be generated automatically

The admin should not manually prepare reports from calls, messages, and spreadsheets.

Reports should come from the data agents already submit:

- Attendance
- Sales visits
- Service updates
- Follow-ups
- Leave requests
- Support requirements

## Outcome

The project turns scattered field activity into a structured daily workflow.

Instead of asking, “What happened in the field today?” the admin can open the app and see it.

Instead of agents sending updates across multiple channels, they have one simple place to save their work.

Instead of reports being a manual end-of-day task, daily, weekly, and monthly summaries can be generated from live field data.

A client had sales and service agents working in the field, but the office did not have a clear view of what was happening during visits.

Updates were scattered across calls, messages, memory, and manual follow-ups.

So we started turning that into a simple mobile-first field app.

Before:
- Field updates were delayed or incomplete

- Leave requests and attendance were separate from reports
- Daily, weekly, and monthly summaries needed manual effort

After:
- Agents log in from their phone
- They check in and check out with location
- Sales and service visits are saved in simple steps
- Leave requests go to admin
- Admin can see person-wise reports and team activity
- Reports can be generated daily, weekly, and monthly

The biggest lesson: if the users are not tech savvy, the product cannot feel like “software.”

It has to feel like the next obvious step in their workday.

AI helped me move from idea to working prototype much faster.

But the real leverage was understanding the business problem clearly enough to guide what should be built.
